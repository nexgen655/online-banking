Be on the lookout for scams

Once you send money, there's little we can do to get it back for you. Here are some red flags of a scam:

  • You're pressured to act quickly.
  • You're asked to deposit a check from someone you never met, then send some or all of the money back out.
  • Your recipient's one account number changes last minute or arrives via email (particularly at home closings).

If you're concerned this transfer may be part of a scam, please Cancel and verify your recipient's account information using a trusted phone number.

If you authorize this transfer, select "Make Transfer", otherwise select "Don't Make Transfer".
